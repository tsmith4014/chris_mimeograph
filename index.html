Chris Duro, intrepid explorer and known for his tenacious travel bug, once decided to journey around the world in his trusty car, affectionately named 'The Globetrotter'.

His adventure began with a curious incident involving a map, a dart, and a blindfold. Wherever the dart landed, that's where he would head. As fate would have it, the dart landed smack dab in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean. "A slight miscalculation," he chuckled, adding pontoons to 'The Globetrotter'.

He drove across continents, through deserts, and even accidentally joined a camel race, which he won, thanks to the car's horsepower - much to the chagrin of the camels.

In Paris, he tried to drive up the Eiffel Tower, mistaking it for a peculiarly structured roundabout. The locals were not amused, but the tourists thought it was a new kind of sightseeing service.

While navigating the serpentine roads of Lombard Street in San Francisco, his GPS, with a rather quirky sense of humor, suggested, "In 300 feet, do a barrel roll." Chris, ever the sport, simply laughed it off and adjusted his rearview mirror, just in case.

After many more misadventures, including a brief stint as a Bollywood stunt driver and an accidental coronation in a small island nation (it's a long story), Chris completed his circumnavigation. 'The Globetrotter', now sporting stickers from around the globe, retired to a quiet life of carrying groceries and the occasional Sunday drive.

As for Chris, he hung up his keys and took up a new hobby: hot air ballooning. Because, why not? The sky's the limit when you've already conquered the road!
